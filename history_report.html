Biography of Abraham Lincoln

Abraham Lincoln came from modest beginnings, being born in a log cabin in 1809. Mainly self-educated, he became a lawyer, and later elected to the Illinois House of Representatives.

He became the 16th president of the United States, when the country tore itself apart due to slavery.

His greatest accomplishments are the 13th Ammendment and winning the Civil War against the South, keeping the country together.
